# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Car owners who know little about cars and are afraid of being ripped off — the person whose check-engine light just came on and whose stomach dropped. They arrive anxious, on a phone, often in a parking lot or their driveway. Secondary: hands-on DIYers who own an OBD2 adapter and want scan-tool-grade data with an expert reading it. The job to be done: "tell me what's actually wrong, what it should cost, and whether I'm about to get taken."

## Product Purpose

Carlos is an AI mechanic that turns a symptom, a fault code, or a live OBD2 capture into a real diagnosis in seconds — ranked causes with confidence, guided repair steps, fair-price estimates, and federal safety data (recalls, TSBs, owner complaints) for the exact vehicle. Success: the user either fixes it themselves (and saves the $100–$180 diagnostic fee) or walks into a shop knowing exactly what to say and what it should cost.

## Positioning

Your advocate against the shop. Every screen reinforces one claim: Carlos is the mechanic on YOUR side — real diagnosis, real data, fair prices, nobody can bullshit you.

## Brand Personality

Carlos is a knowledgeable friend with 20+ years under the hood: direct, honest, never condescending, plain English always (every technical term explained in the same sentence). The interface should deliver wow-factor that stays trustworthy: the impressive moments are the real ones — a diagnosis streaming in live, fuel trims read off the actual engine, 176 federal service bulletins surfacing for your exact car. Calm, precise, quietly extraordinary — a premium scan tool, not a tech demo. Emotional goal on exit: impressed AND in control.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-wrapper (MECH AI): chat-box-with-a-logo, stock dark theme with orange accents, screens that could belong to any AI app.
- Sterile enterprise SaaS: gray dashboards, dense tables, corporate distance.
- Cartoonish or gimmicky: over-mascoted, toy-like, emoji-as-UI. The advice costs real money; it can't feel like a toy.
- Parts-store clutter: AutoZone-style density, banners, walls of SKUs. Carlos gives one clear answer.

## Design Principles

1. **Every wow moment is load-bearing.** Spectacle comes only from real capability — streaming diagnosis, live sensor data, federal records. Decorative flash (gradient confetti, gimmick motion) undercuts trust and is banned.
2. **The receipts are the brand.** Scores come with factor breakdowns, causes come with evidence, estimates come with fair ranges, TSBs come with numbers. Show the working, always.
3. **One clear answer first.** Lead with the verdict (what's wrong, is it safe, what it costs); depth unfolds beneath it. Never make an anxious person hunt.
4. **Plain English is non-negotiable.** Any part named gets its job explained in the same sentence. Jargon is a rip-off vector.
5. **The user's side of the counter.** Every flow ends in leverage: a message to send the shop, a fair price to hold, a fee saved, a question to ask the seller.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Already practiced in code and to be preserved: visible focus states (`:focus-visible`), skip links, `sr-only` labels, `aria-live` for async results, 44px touch targets (gloved hands are a real use case), `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation, AA contrast in both dark and light themes (status colors darken in light mode to hold 4.5:1).
